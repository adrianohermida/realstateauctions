Deadline to Cure Default (Purgar a Mora)

Understand when a property is sent to auction and how the debtor can avoid losing it.

What Is “Purgar a Mora”?

In Brazilian law, *purgar a mora* refers to the debtor’s right to settle overdue payments—along with interest, fines, and contractual charges—before the property is sent to public auction. This procedure allows the debtor to regain full ownership and avoid losing the asset.

What Is Fiduciary Sale?

Fiduciary sale (*alienação fiduciária*) is a legal mechanism commonly used in real estate financing. It involves the transfer of property ownership from the debtor (fiduciante) to the creditor (fiduciário), as a form of guarantee, until the debt is paid in full.

Deadline to Cure Default

In fiduciary sales involving real estate, the debtor has a deadline of 15 calendar days from the date of notification to pay the outstanding debt or negotiate it. In cases involving properties under the Minha Casa, Minha Vida program, the deadline extends to 45 days.

Key Considerations

The right to cure default is a crucial safeguard for debtors facing foreclosure under fiduciary sale. At the same time, potential bidders should always seek up-to-date information on the status of the property before proceeding. Legal due diligence is essential to avoid irreversible losses.

Therefore, both debtors and prospective investors should seek expert legal or real estate guidance to ensure a secure and well-informed auction process.
